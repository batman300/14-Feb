The Life of Rafic Hariri

Timeline of Key Events

1944 - Birth

Rafic Hariri was born in Sidon, Lebanon, into a modest family. His early life in Lebanon shaped his deep connection to his country.

1970 - Business Growth

Hariri moved to Saudi Arabia in the 1970s, where he established a highly successful construction empire, making him one of the wealthiest figures in the Middle East.

1992 - Prime Minister

Following Lebanon's civil war, Hariri was appointed Prime Minister. He played a key role in the country’s reconstruction, especially in revitalizing Beirut.

1994 - Beirut Revitalization

Hariri led the ambitious project to rebuild Beirut, turning it into a bustling center of commerce and tourism.

2000 - Advocating for Change

Hariri was instrumental in calling for the withdrawal of Syrian forces from Lebanon, advocating for political reform and sovereignty.

2005 - Assassination

On February 14, 2005, Hariri was tragically assassinated in a bombing. His death sparked widespread protests and contributed to the Cedar Revolution.

Legacy and Impact

Hariri’s legacy continues to influence Lebanese politics and business. His efforts in rebuilding Lebanon remain pivotal, and his death became a catalyst for change in the nation.
